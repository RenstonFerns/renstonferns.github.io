Welcome to my blog.

I use this space to reflect on the ideas, questions, and challenges that shape my work in clean fuels, circular carbon systems, life cycle assessment, techno-economic analysis, process modelling, and combustion. My research sits at the intersection of engineering design, environmental sustainability, economic feasibility, and real-world fuel deployment — and this blog is where I unpack those connections in a more accessible and reflective way.

The first theme I want to begin with is circular carbon e-fuels. As the world searches for ways to decarbonize transport, liquid fuels remain difficult to replace in many sectors, especially where high energy density, storage stability, and infrastructure compatibility matter. Circular carbon e-fuels offer one possible route forward by using biomass, agricultural residues, captured CO₂, and low-carbon hydrogen to produce fuels such as ethanol, methanol, and e-methanol.

But producing a fuel is only one part of the story. The bigger questions are: Where does the carbon come from? How much energy is required? What are the life-cycle emissions? Can the process be economically viable? How does the fuel perform in an engine? And can these systems be designed in a way that makes sense for specific regions and resources?

Through this blog, I will share short reflections on these questions, along with insights from my research, conference experiences, technical tools, and the broader journey of working at the interface of clean energy, sustainability, and transport fuels.
