Welcome to my blog.

I use this space to reflect on the ideas, questions, and challenges that shape my work in clean fuels, circular carbon systems, life cycle assessment, techno-economic analysis, process modelling, and combustion.

## Why circular carbon e-fuels?

Circular carbon e-fuels offer one possible route forward by using biomass, agricultural residues, captured CO₂, and low-carbon hydrogen to produce fuels such as ethanol, methanol, and e-methanol.

<img src="/uploads/blog/biomass-co2-hydrogen-pathway.jpg" alt="Biomass CO2 hydrogen e-fuel pathway" style="width:100%; border-radius:18px; box-shadow:0 14px 35px rgba(0,0,0,0.25); margin:1.5rem 0;">

But producing a fuel is only one part of the story. The bigger questions are: Where does the carbon come from? How much energy is required? What are the life-cycle emissions? Can the process be economically viable? How does the fuel perform in an engine?

## What to expect from this blog

Through this blog, I will share research reflections, conference experiences, technical explainers, visual notes, and thoughts from my work at the interface of clean energy, sustainability, and transport fuels.
